Differences baked in

  • Hero treatment: the swipe version renders the top pick bigger, with a dish-name gradient and more breathing room. It feels like the answer, not the first item in a list.
  • Context label changes as you swipe: card #1 is "Your table's pick." Cards #2–#5 are "Picks you may like for dinner." Same ranking, just revealed one at a time instead of stacked.
  • Fewer decisions per screen: list UX shows 5 names at once. Swipe UX shows one. Good for "I don't know, suggest something" mode; worse for "let me browse my options."
  • Actions live on the card, not in a sheet: "Go here" / "Not tonight" sit below the deck so the primary decision is always one tap away.
  • Progress pips + "2 of 5": make it clear there's a bounded set — users don't feel like they're committing to an infinite feed.

Try it

  • Drag the top card left/right, or tap Go here / Not tonight.
  • At the end, the deck loops so you can keep exploring the interaction.

Things to stress-test

  • The swipe UX hides ranking. Users who like scanning 5 names at once may feel they've lost agency.
  • An occasion chip change (top of screen) resets the deck — okay, or jarring?
  • A committed-pick card ("Did you end up going?") breaks the single-card rhythm. In the list UX it sits above the hero; in swipe UX, where should it live?
