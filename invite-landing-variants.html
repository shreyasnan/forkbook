Invite landing — showroom approach

Scenario: Pragya sent Shreyas an invite link. He tapped it. Pragya's table is shown as a demo of how ForkBook works — not something he's being granted access to. The primary job of each variant is to move him toward building his own table with his own IRL people.

Design principle: No variant says "join Pragya's table." Each one frames Pragya's content as an example, reassures that ForkBook isn't a public social network, and pushes the CTA toward "build my table."

What changed from v1
In the previous set, variants leaned toward "join Pragya's table" — which, if taken literally, would expose the new user to Pragya's full social graph and break the IRL-only trust model. This v2 set reframes Pragya's data as a showroom: a demo of how ForkBook works, not something the user gets access to. Accepting the invite still creates a mutual 1:1 connection between Shreyas and Pragya — but the landing page narrative is always "build your table," never "enter hers." Each variant includes at least one reassurance about what ForkBook isn't (no strangers, no ads, no public reviews) to make the positioning unmistakable.
