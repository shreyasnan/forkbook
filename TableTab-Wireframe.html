What's changing & why

Subtitle shift

"People whose taste you trust" → "Places your table vouches for."

Signals the tab is about picks, not profiles.

People moved to a gear icon

The 6-person list, trust map, and invite CTA all move into a "Manage your table" sheet behind the ⚙ top-right. They're settings for the engine, not the content.

Frees the main surface for decisions, keeps the social layer one tap away when you actually want it.

Hero = convergence

Top card leads with the rarest, strongest signal your table produces: multiple trusted people pointing at the same place. If no convergence this week, fallback is the single highest-trust pick.

This is the signal only your app can produce. It deserves the biggest pixel.

"From your most-trusted"

3 cards, one dish/place each, ranked by your historical agreement rate with the person — not recency. Proof line is short and warm-accented: name + agreement count.

Same decision-card shape as Home, but weighted through the trust graph instead of editorial curation.

"Worth adding to your list"

Compact list of saves you haven't logged. One-tap + button adds to My Places. No card chrome — it's a lightweight queue, not a browsing surface.

Makes the table productive: things to act on, not things to scroll through.

What's gone

No feed. No "Recent from your table" activity stream. No person-cards as primary content. No trust matrix as a visible row.

Those are social-network patterns. The app's edge is editorial, decision-first, and quiet.
